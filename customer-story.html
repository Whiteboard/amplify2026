From Spreadsheets to Stewardship

When Deloris and her husband founded Glorious Light Community Church, they started with a vision, a handful of faithful members, and an Excel spreadsheet. For years, that spreadsheet was the backbone of every financial operation — tracking tithes, managing budgets, reconciling accounts, and generating reports for the board.

But as the church grew past 200 weekly attendees, the cracks in that manual system became impossible to ignore. Sundays meant paper envelopes, handwritten donation records, and hours of data entry the following week. Reconciliation errors crept in. Donor statements took days to compile. And the finance team — a group of four dedicated volunteers — was spending more time wrestling with numbers than supporting the ministry’s mission.

Something had to change.

Giving Without Walls

The transition to Amplify Giving changed everything. What had been a Sunday-only, cash-and-check operation became a continuous stream of generosity. Members could now give from their phones during service, set up recurring donations from home, or text a gift while watching the livestream.

The shift was immediate and dramatic. Within the first few months, total donations increased by 90%. But the real transformation wasn’t just in the numbers — it was in the culture. Giving was no longer a once-a-week event tied to a physical location. It became a natural, frictionless expression of generosity that happened seven days a week.

First-time givers, who had always felt awkward fumbling with envelopes, found the digital experience welcoming and private. Young families who rarely carried cash could finally participate. And members who traveled or attended online services were no longer excluded from the act of giving.

Real Ministry, Real Impact

The increase in giving didn’t just improve the church’s bottom line — it fueled tangible community transformation. Through their community outreach arm, Timothy Community Corporation, Glorious Light has been able to expand programs that directly serve their neighborhood.

They launched Little Free Libraries throughout the community, putting books into the hands of children and families who need them most. They developed fitness and wellness programs that bring people together around physical health. And they created health education initiatives that address real needs in their community — from nutrition workshops to preventive care awareness.

None of this would have been possible without the financial margin that online giving created. When generosity flows freely, ministry follows.

“Online giving freed us to focus on what matters — serving our community and building the Kingdom.”
— Deloris, Co-Founder, Glorious Light Community Church

Seamless Financial System

The benefits extended well beyond the giving experience itself. By integrating Amplify Giving with Amplify Accounting, Glorious Light eliminated the manual reconciliation process that had consumed so much of their team’s time. Donations now flow directly into the accounting system, automatically categorized by fund, campaign, and donor.

The impact on staffing was significant. The finance team went from four volunteers to two — not because people left, but because the workload shrank so dramatically that two of the four were able to redirect their time to other ministry areas. Reports that once took days to compile are now generated in minutes. Year-end donor statements go out with a few clicks. And the board gets real-time visibility into the church’s financial health.

Advice for Other Churches

When asked what they’d say to other churches still relying on manual processes, the leadership at Glorious Light doesn’t hesitate: start now. The longer you wait, the more time and energy you’re diverting from your actual mission.

Their advice is practical and specific. First, don’t try to change everything at once — start with giving, because the results are immediate and visible. Second, communicate the “why” to your congregation; people embrace change when they understand it’s about better stewardship, not just technology. And third, choose a platform that grows with you, because the needs of a 200-person church are very different from the needs of a 500-person church.

For Glorious Light, the decision to move to Amplify wasn’t just a technology upgrade. It was a ministry decision — one that freed their team, empowered their members, and ultimately transformed their community.
